Afterburner




The faces are drawn from point P0 (the ship's turbine) to Pn, where n is the number of points generated during the time when the afterburner key remained pressed on. The texures (see below) are applied on the faces, and are wrapped until the afterburner stops, always followed by a fading effect (fig. 1).


(fig. 1)

The halo texture (fig. 2) remains at P0, always aligned orthogonally to the observer. The texture is mapped on a face whose color is set in the ship variable spritelight color, and the result of this mapping is that the two colors (the face's and the texture's) are multiplied. The texture color is also modified by the speed of the ship, to further enhance the sense of movement. When the ship is stopped, the texture is almost invisible.
